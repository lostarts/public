How It Began

December 2001. A sheet of veneered plywood balanced across 2x4s in my in-laws' backyard. A circular saw I didn't know how to use. Three turntables and a record collection that needed a home. I made the first cut and ruined it.

At first, the problem seemed obvious. The wrong tools. Inadequate workspace. Not enough experience. But that wasn't the real problem.

What was missing was people. Community. Folks with more experience who could guide me and catch my mistakes. A mentor. A group of peers.

Life has a way of separating artists, makers and other creatives from each other. You move. You change careers. You go freelance. Suddenly the people you used to work alongside aren't around anymore.

Maybe you've been there too. Standing before a project‚Äîa chair, a website, a song, a prototype. You know what you want it to become. You don't know how to get there. You have the tools, space and inspiration. What you're missing are the conversations. The "what if you tried this" from someone who's been where you are. That one piece of advice that unsticks everything.

Where It's Headed

Lost Arts exists for this very reason ‚Äî for studio culture and the messy middle. For the journey from idea to finished work.

It's the community I needed in that backyard. A place to share rough drafts and failed attempts. To ask obvious questions and get real feedback. To find people excited about your mess because they're in the middle of their own.

The truth is simple: making things is easier around other people who make things.

GET INVOLVED

Lost Arts is a portal from your studio to others. See what they're making. Ask questions. Show what you're making. Exchange process and technique, resources and referrels. Not portfolio shots.
